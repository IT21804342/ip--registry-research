About IP-Registry

IP-Registry is an advanced AI-powered decentralized intellectual property protection platform designed to revolutionize how innovations are registered, verified, and compared. It integrates semantic similarity detection, blockchain-based metadata storage, and decentralized document management via IPFS to ensure transparency, authenticity, and security throughout the patent lifecycle.

The system enables inventors, researchers, and organizations to verify novelty, prevent duplication, and establish verifiable ownership ‚Äî all within a single automated ecosystem.

Objectives

The objective of IP-Registry is to develop an AI-powered, blockchain-secured platform that automates patent metadata extraction, performs semantic similarity detection for novelty assessment, and ensures immutable and verifiable registration using Ethereum blockchain and IPFS decentralized storage

The Problem

Traditional patent registration systems are slow, centralized, and prone to human error. Manual verification and keyword-based searches often fail to detect semantically similar patents, leading to duplication, ownership disputes, and lack of transparency in intellectual property management.

Our Solution

IP-Registry introduces a comprehensive AI-driven and blockchain-secured platform for patent registration and novelty verification.

Literature Review


A review of global and local research highlighting the importance of ADAS and IP-Registry‚Äôs innovative approach to road safety.

Intellectual Property (IP) protection has evolved significantly with advances in artificial intelligence and blockchain technologies. Conventional patent management systems rely heavily on manual verification and centralized databases, which often lead to inefficiencies, delayed registrations, and vulnerability to data tampering. The increasing volume of patent filings worldwide necessitates automation in novelty detection and secure ownership verification.

Driver Identification and Monitoring systems play a crucial role in ensuring that only authorized individuals operate a vehicle while also maintaining driver attention and alertness throughout the journey. Studies have demonstrated the effectiveness of monitoring driver behavior to detect distraction and drowsiness using machine learning. However, these systems often fail to address driver identity verification. In contrast, biometric identification methods such as facial recognition and voice authentication contribute to secure access but are limited in providing continuous verification throughout a trip.

Patent Similarity and Semantic Retrieval Early studies on patent retrieval primarily depended on keyword-based and vector-space models such as TF-IDF, which lacked the ability to understand contextual or semantic relationships between terms [1]. With the emergence of transformer-based architectures such as BERT and Sentence-BERT, contextual embeddings improved the precision of semantic similarity matching [2], [3]. Models like LaBSE (Language-agnostic BERT Sentence Embedding) extended this capability across multiple languages, enabling multilingual patent searches [4]. However, most research still focuses on general text benchmarks rather than real-world patent corpora that contain long, domain-specific claims. To address this, IP-Registry employs Sentence-Transformer LaBSE models to generate high-dimensional embeddings, coupled with FAISS for efficient vector similarity indexing and retrieval.

Efficient Vector Search and Indexing Efficient retrieval of semantically similar patents requires approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) algorithms that can scale to millions of embeddings. Tools such as Facebook AI Similarity Search (FAISS) have been widely used for dense vector indexing using clustering methods like Inverted File (IVF) and Product Quantization (PQ) [5]. Research demonstrates that FAISS can achieve sub-second search performance on large datasets with minimal accuracy loss. IP-Registry integrates FAISS as its backbone for semantic retrieval, enabling real-time novelty assessment through cosine similarity thresholds.

Patent PDF Parsing and Metadata Extraction

Patent documents are typically unstructured and often appear as scanned PDFs. Literature on document digitization highlights hybrid approaches that combine Optical Character Recognition (OCR) with text-layout extraction to improve reliability [6]. Frameworks such as PyMuPDF and Tesseract OCR have been proven effective in parsing multi-column legal documents and preserving semantic sections. Building on this, IP-Registry incorporates a two-stage extractor that first retrieves digital text using PyMuPDF and falls back to Tesseract-based OCR for image-only pages. Heuristic logic is then applied to identify key sections such as Title, Abstract, and Claims, which are crucial for similarity computation and metadata registration.

Blockchain-Based IP Provenance

The use of blockchain for intellectual property provenance and digital rights management has gained significant attention. Studies have explored decentralized IP registries using Ethereum smart contracts to ensure immutability, time-stamping, and transparent ownership verification [7], [8]. Researchers emphasize the benefits of on-chain proof-of-existence while storing large content off-chain to reduce cost [9]. IP-Registry follows this hybrid architecture: the extracted metadata is stored on the Ethereum Sepolia test network via a custom smart contract (PatentStorage.sol), while the full patent documents are stored on the InterPlanetary File System (IPFS) for decentralized persistence.

Decentralized Storage and IPFS Integration

Decentralized storage solutions such as IPFS have been identified as reliable systems for verifiable, content-addressable file management [10]. Studies show that IPFS provides strong data integrity and tamper resistance by linking files through cryptographic hashes. Projects combining IPFS with blockchain enable a trusted bridge between off-chain files and on-chain metadata, ensuring both scalability and transparency. IP-Registry leverages Pinata as a pinning service to guarantee long-term file availability, providing verifiable document access through gateway URLs embedded within blockchain transactions.

Identified Research Gaps

The literature reveals that while AI-based patent retrieval and blockchain registration have each been studied extensively, an end-to-end integration combining AI-powered similarity detection, decentralized storage, and verifiable blockchain registration remains underexplored. Most existing works evaluate components in isolation rather than as part of a unified IP protection ecosystem. Additionally, operational aspects such as scalability, latency, and user-level interpretability are rarely addressed.

Summary

The reviewed literature establishes the foundational techniques that underpin IP-Registry‚Äîsemantic text embedding for contextual similarity, FAISS-based vector indexing for scalable retrieval, OCR-assisted metadata extraction for real-world documents, and blockchain with IPFS for immutable storage. By combining these technologies, IP-Registry closes a critical research gap, delivering a decentralized, intelligent, and verifiable intellectual property protection platform that aligns with modern demands for transparency, efficiency, and automation.
